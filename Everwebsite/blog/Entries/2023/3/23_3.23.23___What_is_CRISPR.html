What is CRISPR?

CRISPR stands for "Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats" and refers to a naturally occurring immune system in bacteria that allows them to defend against invading viruses by cutting out and destroying viral DNA.

Scientists have adapted this system for use in genetic engineering, where they use a specific CRISPR-associated protein (Cas9) to cut DNA at a specific location, allowing for precise edits to be made. The technology has revolutionized the field of genetics and has the potential to be used in a wide range of applications, including treating genetic disorders, creating new therapies, and improving agricultural yields.
